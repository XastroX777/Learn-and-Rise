Our Mission

Learn & Rise is dedicated to providing quality education to children aged 6 to 14 from low-income or remote communities with limited access to traditional schooling. We transform available community spaces into vibrant learning environments, combining traditional teaching with innovative digital content.

How It Works

1

Setting Up Community Micro-Schools

We convert available community spaces such as halls, places of worship, or unused shops into simple classrooms. Each school starts with a small group (10‚Äì20 students) to make management easier and ensure quality education.

2

Training Local Teachers

Educated local youth are selected and trained to become community teachers. They receive ongoing support through teaching tools and periodic training to maintain educational quality.

3

Hybrid Learning Model

Traditional classroom instruction is combined with offline digital content using tablets or portable servers. Subjects include literacy, numeracy, and practical life skills like hygiene, teamwork, and financial awareness.

4

Flexible Learning Schedules

Classes are held at different times (morning or evening) to accommodate children who work or help their families. Instead of rigid age-based grades, the system focuses on each child's learning progress.

5

Community & Family Involvement

Awareness sessions and adult literacy classes are offered to parents. Enrolled children receive incentives like free meals or basic health checkups to encourage attendance.

Required Resources

Human Resources

  • Trained local teachers
  • One field coordinator per region
  • Visiting trainers for tech or assessments

Physical Resources

  • Free or low-rent community spaces
  • Basic furniture: tables, mats, blackboards
  • Basic school supplies

Educational & Digital Resources

  • Tablets or basic laptops (with solar chargers if needed)
  • Offline educational content such as Khan Academy and Kolibri
  • Printed books, stories, and hands-on activity materials

Financial Resources

  • Initial funding from NGOs or social responsibility grants
  • Small family contributions after a free trial period
  • Donations of devices and books from charities or individual sponsors
